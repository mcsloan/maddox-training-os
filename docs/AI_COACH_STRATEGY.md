# AI Coach Strategy

## Principle

AI Coach is a future intelligence layer, not a gimmick. It should only be added after the data model, sync behavior, projections, and QA guardrails are trustworthy.

## Maddox-Facing Behavior

AI output for Maddox should be:

- short
- simple
- clear
- age-appropriate
- no pressure or shame
- focused on one or two useful cues

Example:

"Today is a lacrosse load day. Keep stick work light and sharp."

## Parent-Facing Behavior

AI output for the parent should:

- explain what data means
- recommend adjustments
- flag risk
- summarize trends
- identify missing evidence
- help decide what to do tomorrow

## Guardrails

- AI recommends; parent approves.
- AI does not silently rewrite the plan.
- AI uses approved source documents and logged data.
- AI never encourages overtraining.
- AI prioritizes recovery and age-appropriate development.
- Pain/soreness overrides ambition.
- AI must distinguish missing data from explicitly deferred data.

## Future Inputs

- Canonical Day evidence
- Sport Load logs
- Training Work logs
- KPI trends
- Readiness/recovery data
- Parent notes
- Approved v8.4 plan data
- Source video/instruction coverage

## Deferred Until

- KPI cloud sync is stable.
- Staging environment exists.
- History is Week -> Day -> Evidence.
- Rest/recovery day states are explicit.
- App-wide sync visibility exists.
- QA/release gate is in place.
